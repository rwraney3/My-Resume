    Summary

    In 1999, I began my career as a fund raiser. In April of 2007, I passed the examination to become a Certified Fund Raising Executive (CFRE). Sadly, in 2019, those credentials expired. In my 19 years in the business, I worked for a private Seventh-day Adventist Academy (Union Springs Academy), a private Seventh-day Adventist University (Southern Adventist University), and a ministry that helps meet the physical, educaitonal, and spiritual needs of disadvantaged children living in Bangladesh, India, Nepal, Myanmar, and Sri Lanka (Child Impact International).

    Since leaving Child Impact International in May 2018, I have worked in the Gig Economy (Uber, Lyft, GrubHub, Shipt, and Instacart). I worked as a general laborer doing contruction - painting, carpenter's assistant, and operating equipment (mini-excavator, skid-steer, and John Deere tractor) on the job site.

    In October, I started taking online courses to learn web development tools.

    Although, my experiences since leaving Child Impact International seem to be a step away from fund raising, I see them as a tool in my belt as a communicator. I believe, my work history (farms, broomshops, plastic factories, McDonalds, driving instructor, school bus driver) has made me more able to carry on meaningful conversations with benefactors. It has made me willing to "get my hands dirty" in order to gain trust and be successful.
